2.  Rosetta Network Structure

The structure of the network is as follows:

  1.  Researchers \(r_{1}, r_{2},...,r_{\left[ R\right]} \in R.\)

  1.  Research items \(t_{1}, t_{2},...,t_{\left[ T\right]} \in T.\)

    Each researcher has an associated set of research items which they have (co-)authored, and each research item has an associated set of researcher(s) who (co-)authored it. Suppose that researcher \(r\) has a set of \(n\) research items. Then we denote \(r\)’s research items as \(r^{1}, r^{2},...r^{n}\), and and refer to the collection of research items as \(r^{*}\) . Likewise, suppose that research item \(t\) has \(m\) authors. Then we denote \(t\)’s authors as \(t^{1}, t^{2},...t^{m}\) and refer to all the authors of \(t\) and \(t^{*}\).
